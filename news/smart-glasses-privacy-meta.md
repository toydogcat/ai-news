    <span v-if="displayMode !== 'en'" class="zh-text">隱私災難還是科技未來？Meta 智慧眼鏡熱銷背後的「無聲偷拍」隱憂</span>
    <span v-if="displayMode !== 'zh'" class="en-text">Smart Glasses Are 'An Invasion of Privacy' - Meta's Are Selling Better Than Ever</span>
  </h1>

<div style="text-align: center; margin: 24px 0 32px 0;">
  <img :src="'/ai-news/smart-glasses-privacy-meta/hero.png'" alt="Meta Smart Glasses Privacy" style="border-radius: 16px; max-width: 100%; box-shadow: 0 8px 32px rgba(0,0,0,0.15); border: 1px solid rgba(255,255,255,0.08);" />
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">新一代「智慧眼鏡」所引發的社會爭議似乎正在排山倒海而來。</p>
  <p class="en-text">Issues with a new wave of "smart glasses" seem to be piling up.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">然而，即便隱憂重重，全球頂尖的科技巨頭們仍雄心勃勃，準備在未來數年內向大眾售出數以千萬計的此類產品。</p>
  <p class="en-text">Yet some of the biggest technology companies in the world are poised to sell many millions of pairs in the coming years.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">在海灘嬉戲後離場的女性、走進零售店購物的顧客，或僅僅是站在路邊的行人，正頻頻遭遇佩戴 Meta Ray-Ban 智慧眼鏡男子的搭訕。這些男子往往在對方完全不知情或未徵得同意的情況下，秘密錄下女性對隨意提問或搭訕詞的真實反應。</p>
  <p class="en-text">Women leaving the beach, going into a shop, or simply standing outside are now being approached by men usually wearing Meta's Ray-Bans, the company's "smart" or "AI" glasses, often in order to film the women's responses to casual questions or pick-up lines without their knowledge or consent.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">許多女性直到這些影片在網路上瘋傳並招致惡言酸語時，才驚覺自己已被偷拍。由於在公共場所拍照在法律上通常被認定為合法，受害者幾乎投訴無門。一名受害女性告訴 BBC，當她要求上傳者撤下未經授權的偷拍影片時，對方竟然厚顏宣稱「刪除影片是付費服務」。</p>
  <p class="en-text">The women only find out about the videos of them after they gain traction, and often abuse, online. They have little legal recourse as photography in public is broadly considered legal. One woman told the BBC that when she asked the person who posted a secret recording of her to remove it, she was told that doing so was "a paid service".</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">Meta 的智慧眼鏡目前穩坐市場龍頭，估計囊括了全球超過 80% 的 AI 智慧眼鏡銷量，這主要歸功於該公司是近年來首家推出此類革命性產品的科技巨擘。</p>
  <p class="en-text">Meta's glasses are currently the most popular on the market, estimated to make up more than 80% of all AI or smart glasses sales, as the company was the first major tech player to launch such a product in recent years.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">這款與全球眼鏡龍頭依視路陸遜梯卡（EssilorLuxottica）聯手打造的產品，完美承襲了 Ray-Ban 的經典復古外型。然而，其鏡框中卻隱藏著幾乎肉眼難辨的微型鏡頭，鏡腿處設有小型揚聲器，且鏡片能向佩戴者投射數位資訊。使用者只需隨意輕觸一下鏡框，就能無聲無息地開始錄製影片或拍攝照片。</p>
  <p class="en-text">Made in partnership with EssilorLuxottica and offering the classic look of Ray-Bans, the glasses feature an almost invisible camera in the frames, small speakers in the arms, and lenses that can show a wearer some information. People can start recording video or take a photo with a casual touch of the frames.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">由於 Meta 眼鏡鏡頭的偽裝性極高，甚至連佩戴者本身都有時會對「何時在錄影、拍到了什麼，以及這些隱私畫面最終流向何方」感到措手不及。</p>
  <p class="en-text">The nature of the camera in Meta's glasses can be so unobtrusive that even their wearers have been caught off guard by what and when they're recording, and where those recordings are going.</p>
</div>

## ⚖️ 肯亞審核員醜聞與集體訴訟 (Scandals and Lawsuits)

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">先前，受僱於肯亞的數據審核員（負責檢視智慧眼鏡拍攝的影像以訓練 Meta 的 AI 模型）爆料稱，他們被迫觀看大量露骨的私密畫面——包括性行為和使用者上廁所的影像。這隨即引發智慧眼鏡持有者的極大恐慌，並催生了兩起集體訴訟。在一宗訴訟中，車主表示根本不知道有錄下此類影片；在另一宗中，使用者則指責公司在未告知的情況下，將他們的個人生活影片交予人工審核。</p>
  <p class="en-text">After workers in Kenya, tasked with watching videos made through Meta's glasses to create AI training data for the company, said they were being required to watch graphic content like sex and bathroom usage, people who own the glasses filed two lawsuits. In one, people said they had no idea such videos had been made. In the other, they said they did not know their videos were being shared by the company for review.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">Meta 先前對此回應稱，其服務條款中已明確告知用戶，在某些情況下其內容可能會接受人工審核。</p>
  <p class="en-text">Meta has previously said that users were made aware of the possibility of human review in some circumstances in its terms of service.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">即便如此，銷量依舊勢如破竹。據該公司統計，至今已賣出超過 700 萬副眼鏡。</p>
  <p class="en-text">Nevertheless, sales continue to rise. Today, seven million pairs and counting have been sold, according to the company.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">「這絕對是人類歷史上成長最快的消費性電子產品之一，」Meta 執行長馬克·祖克柏（Mark Zuckerberg）在今年早些時候如此自豪宣稱。</p>
  <p class="en-text">"They're some of the fastest-growing consumer electronics in history," Mark Zuckerberg, Meta's chief executive, boasted earlier this year.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">Meta 發言人崔西·克萊頓（Tracy Clayton）則向 BBC 表示，公眾應當以負責任的態度使用任何科技產品。「我們有專職團隊致力於防範與打擊濫用，但如同任何科技，最終的義務依然落在個人身上，使用者不應刻意利用它去剝削他人。」</p>
  <p class="en-text">Tracy Clayton, a Meta spokesman, told the BBC that people should behave responsibly with any technology. "We have teams dedicated to limiting and combating misuse, but as with any technology, the onus is ultimately on individual people to not actively exploit it."</p>
</div>

## 🚀 科技巨頭的下一個萬億市場 (The Next Billion-Dollar Market)

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">如今，其他科技帝國也準備強勢進軍這個被視為「智慧型手機後」下一個劃時代的終極產品類別。</p>
  <p class="en-text">Now, other major tech companies are planning to get in on what may have the potential to be the tech industry's long-awaited new product category.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">據傳，蘋果（Apple）正緊鑼密鼓地研發自家的智慧眼鏡，預計最快將於明年問世。Snap 亦宣佈將在今年推出其沉浸式眼鏡「Specs」的全新一代版本。</p>
  <p class="en-text">Apple is reportedly developing its own version of smart glasses, possibly to be released next year. Snap has said it will release a new version of its smart glasses, called Specs, this year.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">就連谷歌（Google）也摩拳擦掌準備捲土重來。十多年前其推出的「谷歌眼鏡（Google Glass）」因隱私爭議和昂貴定價慘遭滑鐵盧，並在上市兩年內夭折；如今，他們也將重新佈局這一賽道。</p>
  <p class="en-text">Google, too, is set to try again with smart glasses, more than a decade after its notorious Google Glass flop, which the company pulled from the public within two years of launch as the pricey gadget came under fire over privacy concerns.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">所有這些新品預期都將結合人工智慧（AI）與擴增實境（AR）技術，而這類功能不可避免地需要一個無處不在的鏡頭。</p>
  <p class="en-text">All are expected to offer some combination of artificial intelligence (AI) and augmented reality (AR) technology, as Meta's glasses do, which typically requires a camera.</p>
</div>

## 🎧 兩面刃：便利生活 vs. 隱私崩壞 (The Two Sides of the Device)

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">當然，公眾使用智慧眼鏡的方式也不全然是負面的。馬克·史密斯（Mark Smith）每天都會戴上他的 Meta Ray-Bans 出門。</p>
  <p class="en-text">The way people may use the coming wave of smart glasses will not be all bad, of course. Mark Smith wears his Meta Ray-Bans every day.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">「我戴著它們走遍世界各地。基礎功能非常出色，」身為顧問公司 ISG 合夥人的史密斯是一位精通科技的「早期採用者」。但令他對眼鏡愛不釋手的原因，與技術上的飛躍無關，而是在於極致的生活體驗。</p>
  <p class="en-text">"I've used them around the world, in all kinds of places. The basic features are great," Smith said. As a partner at the advisory firm ISG where he focuses on enterprise software, Smith can be classified as a tech-savvy early adopter.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">他喜歡在洗碗時戴著它聽音樂或 Podcast，因為這不會像耳塞式耳機那樣隔絕周遭聲音，且接聽電話也非常輕鬆。旅行時，再也不用手忙腳亂地掏出手機，只消一個眼神和微調，就能隨手捕捉美景。</p>
  <p class="en-text">He likes to wear them while washing up the dishes at home because they make it easy for him to listen to music or a podcast without blocking out other noise like most headphones do. Taking phone calls through the glasses is a breeze. When travelling, it's nice not to have to constantly pull out his phone to snap a quick picture or video.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">但史密斯也承認，隱私漏洞再明顯不過。錄影時自動亮起的那顆指示小燈在日光下非常微弱，幾乎不會被旁人注意到。絕大多數人根本不知道他臉上戴著的，是一部具有連網攝影功能的精密儀器。</p>
  <p class="en-text">Even so, Smith said some potential privacy issues are obvious. The small light that turns on when the glasses are recording appears dim in daylight and often goes unnoticed, he said. Most people seem to have no idea he's wearing anything other than normal eyeglasses.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">研究機構預估，若未來數家巨頭的推廣順利，未來幾年內將有多達 1 億人購買並配戴智慧眼鏡。</p>
  <p class="en-text">Should AI or smart glasses products from more companies end up selling as well as Meta's version, researchers expect as many as 100 million people will buy a pair in the next few years.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">若此預測成真，那麼法院、博物館、電影院、醫院以及公廁等向來嚴禁攝影的公共場所，要如何防範與執行法規將面臨空前挑戰——因為這意味著「眼鏡即相機」。</p>
  <p class="en-text">If such a prediction becomes reality, the ability of institutions to enforce norms and laws that typically prohibit recording in places like courthouses, museums, movie theatres, hospitals and bathrooms will be difficult when suddenly millions of eyeglasses are also cameras.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">美國隱私權執業律師大衛·凱斯勒（David Kessler）指出，許多企業客戶已為此頭痛不已。「未來情勢可能會發展得很黑暗，」凱斯勒說。「我絕非反對科技的頑固派，但從社會層面來看……難道未來我只要一踏入公共場所，就必須無時無刻提防自己被偷錄嗎？」</p>
  <p class="en-text">David Kessler, an attorney who heads the US privacy practice at Norton Rose Fulbright, said many of his corporate clients are already having to grapple with this. "There are some pretty dark places we could go here," Kessler said. "I'm not anti-technology in any sense, but as a societal matter...will I need to think [of being recorded] anytime I go out in public?"</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">更令人膽寒的是，據報 Meta 正計劃在下一代眼鏡中植入「人臉識別技術（Facial Recognition）」。這意味著佩戴者不但能在暗中錄影，還能瞬間起底路人的真實身分。</p>
  <p class="en-text">And Meta reportedly plans to add facial recognition technology in an updated version of its glasses, meaning wearers could not only have the ability to surreptitiously record anyone, but quickly identify them, as well.</p>
</div>

## ❌ 當科技逾越了道德的界線 (Ignored Guidelines and Public Backlash)

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">儘管 Meta 以「為隱私而生，由你掌控」的標語宣傳，並在指南中建議「若他人拒絕，請勿錄影」、「身處敏感場所請關閉設備」。但現實是，這些軟性的道德呼籲正形同虛設。</p>
  <p class="en-text">Meta markets its glasses under the tagline: "Designed for privacy, controlled by you." It suggests to users of the glasses that they do not record people who state they do not want to be recorded, and that users turn the glasses off completely "in sensitive spaces". Those suggestions often seem to go ignored.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">在社交媒體上，用智慧眼鏡惡搞素人正蔚為流行。許多年輕男性配戴著眼鏡誘騙路人簽署惡搞請願書，或拿噴灑了惡臭化學劑的蠟燭讓店員試聞，甚至有網紅戴著它去速食店得來速搶奪食物後狂奔，全程以第一人稱視角高清直播錄影。</p>
  <p class="en-text">An increasingly popular use of the glasses is to record pranks on unsuspecting people. Wearers, often young men, get people to sign fake petitions or retail workers to smell candles sprayed with bad odours. Sometimes they steal food as it's being handed over at a drive-thru and record their sprint.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">大眾一旦意識到身邊的人配戴的是智慧眼鏡，防衛本能會立刻被喚醒。時尚網紅阿尼耶莎·娜瓦蘿（Aniessa Navarro）透露，當她在接受私密的蜜蠟除毛服務時，猛然發現美容師戴的竟然是 Meta 眼鏡，這讓她感到毛骨悚然、甚至反胃。雖然該名美容師堅稱眼鏡沒電也沒錄影，只是需要用來配戴近視鏡片，但裂痕已難以彌補。</p>
  <p class="en-text">People do often recoil when they discover a person is wearing smart glasses. Online influencer Aniessa Navarro said she felt sick when she realised during a personal waxing session that her technician was wearing Meta's glasses. The technician said they weren't charged or recording, and that she needed to wear them for the prescription lenses.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">當 Meta 技術長安德魯·博斯沃思（Andrew Bosworth）在 Instagram 上被問到「每日配戴智慧眼鏡在公共場合所面臨的道德污名」時，他卻避重就輕地回應：從驚人的銷售量看來，這代表產品已「被廣大群眾所接受」。</p>
  <p class="en-text">Andrew Bosworth, Meta's chief technology officer, was asked on Instagram two weeks ago about "the stigma around people wearing smart glasses every day". He responded by saying that the sheer number of Meta Ray-Bans sold "suggest that these are widely accepted".</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">曾任 Meta AI 研究員、現任職加州大學柏克萊分校且擔任美歐 AI 政策顧問的大衛·哈里斯（David Harris）持相反論調。他指出這代眼鏡將無可避免地遭遇與谷歌眼鏡當年相同的終局反彈：「這類科技在根源上就是對個人隱私的公然侵犯，隨著它越來越普及，社會輿論的大爆發與強烈抵制將無可避免。」</p>
  <p class="en-text">But David Harris, a former Meta AI researcher who now teaches at UC Berkeley and is an adviser on AI policy in the US and EU, said he expects this generation of AI smart glasses to run up against the same issues that doomed the Google Glass more than a decade ago. "Technology like this is fundamentally an invasion of privacy and it's really going to face more and more backlash," he said.</p>
</div>

<div class="bilingual-paragraph">
  <p class="zh-text">抵制的火苗早已引燃。去年 12 月，一名男子在網上氣憤發文，指責他在紐約地鐵上因用 Meta 眼鏡偷錄一名女性，結果被該女性憤怒奪下當場摔爛。然而，他沒有在網路上博得任何同情，成千上萬的網友反倒齊聲高呼該名女性是「反擊偷窺的真英雄」。</p>
  <p class="en-text">More signs of such a backlash are starting to appear. In December a man posted a video complaining that a woman he'd been recording on the New York City Subway broke his Meta glasses. If he was expecting sympathy he was wrong. The internet hailed her as a hero.</p>
</div>
